## Reading Notes: Design and Analysis of a Watermarking System for Care Labels
### Ragan-Kelley & Tran (Pacific-Rim Conference on Multimedia, 2007)

> **Summary:** This paper proposes a way to hide clothing care instructions directly inside a fabric's printed design, instead of using a separate physical tag. The idea is that a smart washer or dryer could someday read hidden instructions straight from fabric pattern. The authors hide care label using a watermarking method that slightly changes certain frequency values inside image, based on a technique called discrete cosine transform. They prove two mathematical results assuming pixel values behave randomly with a fixed average and spread. First, they calculate expected amount of visible distortion watermark causes. Second, they calculate an upper limit on how much a simulated round of fabric fading can disturb hidden watermark. They then test system on nine sample images and confirm that a moderate amount of backup data and a mild watermark strength keeps image looking normal while still surviving repeated fading, in most cases even after image has faded to near white.
